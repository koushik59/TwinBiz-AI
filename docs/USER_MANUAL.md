# 📖 TwinBiz AI — User Manual

**Welcome!** This guide walks you through everything TwinBiz AI can do, in plain language.
Read it once before you start (15 minutes), and keep it handy as a reference.

---

## 1. What is TwinBiz AI?

Imagine having an exact copy of your shop inside your computer — same products, same
customers, same sales pattern. Now imagine you could **test your business decisions on the
copy first**: raise a price, hire a person, launch a new product — and see what would
happen *before* spending a single real rupee.

That copy is called a **digital twin**, and that's what TwinBiz AI builds for you.

> **The golden rule of TwinBiz:** every number you see is calculated from *your* business
> data, and every prediction explains itself. Predictions are clearly labeled
> *"Predicted, not guaranteed"* — they are smart estimates, not promises.

## 2. Getting started

### Creating your account
1. Open the website and click **Get Started** (or **Sign in** if you have an account).
2. Register with your name, email and a password (minimum 6 characters).

### Want to explore first? Use the demo
On the login page you can enter with the demo account — a fully loaded supermarket
("SmartMart") with one full year of realistic history. Everything in this manual can be
tried there safely.

### The Setup Wizard — creating your twin
After registering, a short 3-step wizard builds your twin:

1. **About your business** — name, type (Supermarket, Restaurant, Pharmacy, Bakery…) and location.
2. **Your scale** — roughly how many employees, customers, monthly revenue and expenses you
   have. Don't worry about exact figures; you can change them later in Settings.
3. **How do you want to start?** Choose one:
   - **Use Demo Supermarket** — instantly get 365 days of realistic sample data so every
     chart and prediction works from minute one. Sample data is always labeled **DEMO** in
     the app, and you can replace it with real data later.
   - **Upload Real Business Data** — start empty and bring in your own Excel/CSV files
     (see *Data Center*, section 12).
   - **Manual Setup** — start empty and add products by hand.

That's it — you land on your Dashboard.

## 3. Dashboard — your morning glance ☕

**What it shows:** the state of your whole business on one screen.

- **The status strip (top):** tells you whether your twin is running on **DEMO** data,
  **LIVE** data, or has gone **STALE** (no recent data). It also shows a *data quality*
  and a *confidence* percentage — the more complete and recent your data, the more you can
  trust the predictions.
- **Big number tiles:** this month's revenue, profit, expenses and customers — each with a
  small arrow showing whether it's up or down versus last month.
- **Business Health gauge:** a single 0–100 score summarising finance, inventory,
  operations, customers and marketing. Above 70 is good; below 50 needs attention.
- **Charts:** revenue vs expenses over 30/90 days, weekly profit, customer footfall,
  your top-selling products, and your busiest hours.
- **Upcoming Risks & AI Suggestions:** the two most useful widgets — problems the twin has
  spotted and what it suggests doing about them.

**How to use it:** open it every morning like a newspaper. Green arrows — carry on.
Red arrows or a risk warning — click through to the page it points to.

## 4. Digital Twin — walk through your virtual shop 🏪

**What it shows:** your shop drawn as an interactive map — departments, shelves (one card
per product), your team, and your suppliers.

Shelf colors tell the story at a glance:
- 🟢 **Green** — healthy stock
- 🟡 **Yellow** — running low, reorder soon
- 🔴 **Red** — critical, about to run out
- 🟠 **Orange** — overstocked (money sitting idle on the shelf)

**How to use it:** click anything. Click a shelf → see that product's margin, days of stock
remaining, and a tip like *"order 140 units now to cover 30 days."* Click an employee or a
supplier → see their key numbers too.

---

## 5. Products — your catalog 📦

**What it does:** manage everything you sell — name, brand, price, cost, stock level, tax,
supplier, shelf life and more.

**How to use it:**
- **Search and filter** by name, brand, or category; sort by price, stock, etc.
- **Add Product** — only name, price and cost are truly required. If you skip the SKU
  (product code), one is generated for you automatically.
- **Edit / Delete** with the icons on each row.
- The **stock status** column uses the same green/yellow/red/orange language as the
  Digital Twin.

## 6. AI Business Simulator — test decisions safely 🧪

**This is the heart of TwinBiz.** Eight sliders, each one a real business decision:

price, marketing budget, number of employees, discounts, inventory purchases, store hours,
supplier cost, and operating expenses.

**How to use it:**
1. Move any slider — say, price **+10%**.
2. Instantly see the predicted result: revenue, profit, customers, satisfaction, cash flow,
   and a risk score — side by side with your current numbers, plus a plain-English verdict
   like *"⚠️ this reduces profit."*
3. Expand **"How was this predicted?"** under any result to see the reasoning in
   plain words (e.g. *"supermarket customers are price-sensitive — a 10% increase typically
   costs ~17% of demand"*).
4. Like an outcome? Click **Save as Scenario** to keep it for comparison.

**Quick What-Ifs:** one-click presets — *"Increase milk price by ₹2"*, *"Hire one extra
cashier"*, *"Launch 10% festival offer"* — already ranked by predicted profit impact.

**Product Price mode:** switch modes to test a price change on **one specific product**
(e.g. Amul Milk ₹28 → ₹30) and see predicted demand, profit and the risk that customers
switch to another brand.

## 7. Scenarios — compare your saved plans ⚖️

**What it does:** puts your saved simulator experiments side by side with your current
business: revenue, profit, expenses, customers, satisfaction, risk and health in one table.

The best value in each row is highlighted, the overall winner gets a crown 👑, and separate
badges mark *best profit*, *best growth*, *lowest risk*, and the *AI-recommended* balance of
all three. Delete old scenarios with the trash icon.

---

## 8. Forecasting — see the weeks ahead 📈

**What it does:** predicts your revenue, customers, orders or expenses for the next
14 to 90 days, using a machine-learning model trained on your own history. It learns your
weekly rhythm — if Sundays are big for you, the forecast knows.

**How to read the chart:**
- **Solid line** — what actually happened.
- **Dashed line** — the prediction.
- **Shaded band** — the realistic range (the future usually lands inside it).
- **Model confidence** — how well the model fits your history. Higher = more reliable.

---

## 9. AI Advisor — ask anything 🤖

**What it does:** a chat assistant that knows *your* numbers. Ask things like:
- *"Should I hire another employee?"*
- *"What should I do today?"*
- *"Should I launch this product?"*

Every answer is grounded in your live business data and comes structured as: explanation →
prediction → recommendation → risk to watch → confidence level. A small badge shows the
source of the answer. Your chat history is saved.

## 10. Product Launch Lab — test a product before you stock it 🚀

**The problem it solves:** buying stock for a new product is a gamble. The Launch Lab lets
you run the whole launch inside the twin first.

**How to use it:**
1. **Create an experiment** — describe the product: costs (supplier, transport, wastage…),
   the price range you'd consider, planned stock, marketing budget, target customers, shelf
   placement, and a competitor's price if you know it. (Try the one-click **demo product**
   first to see how it works.)
2. **Price Sweep** — tests every price in your range and shows the best price for profit,
   for adoption, and the balanced recommendation.
3. **Discount Lab** — what launch offer (0–20%) pays off.
4. **Inventory Lab** — how many units to stock first, so you neither sell out nor sit on
   dead stock.
5. **Find Best Launch Strategy** — the AI searches every combination of price, discount,
   stock and marketing within your limits and hands you the best plan.
6. **Launch Analysis** — the full picture: a 0–100 launch risk score, break-even timeline,
   the best launch window (festivals considered), and **cannibalization** — plain meaning:
   how much the new product will "steal" from your existing similar products.
7. **"Should I Launch This Product?"** — the final verdict: YES / CONDITIONAL YES / WAIT /
   HIGH RISK, with the reason and a concrete recommendation.

Save promising configurations as launch scenarios and compare them, exactly like the
Simulator's scenarios.

## 11. The AI Lab — six intelligence features 🧠

Found in the sidebar under **AI Lab**. These read your history and turn it into insight.

### 🕰 Time Machine
Travel to any past date and see your business as it was that day — sales, cash, top
sellers — with AI explanations of what happened: *"Revenue spiked +43% — Holi festival
window"* or *"Milk had no sales for 5 days — likely stockout, about 100 lost units."*
Drag the slider or use the **-90d / -30d / -7d** shortcuts.

At the bottom: **Tomorrow's Headlines** — playful newspaper headlines about your *future*,
written from your real forecast (*"Store Faces Stockouts During Diwali Rush"*). They're
clearly labeled as illustrations — if a headline looks bad, it names the fix.

### 👑 AI CEO Mode
The AI drafts real, specific decisions for you — *"Order 168 units of Lays Chips"*,
*"Raise Fortune Oil to ₹187 (worth about +₹2,100/month)"* — each with its reasoning and
predicted value. **Nothing happens until you click Approve.** Approving a price change
applies it instantly; approving a restock records the order for you to place with your
supplier. Rejected ideas stay away for two weeks. The History tab is your decision diary.

### ⛅ Business Weather
Your next 7 or 14 days as a weather report. Three readings per day — **Sales** (will it be
a good day?), **Inventory** (⛈ on the exact day something sells out), **Cash** (will the
day end positive?). One glance replaces five charts: a storm icon means act today.

### 🛡 Stress Test
What if a supplier fails? Staff don't show up? Prices inflate 15%? Six crisis scenarios run
against your real numbers and answer: *how much would it cost per month, how long could you
survive it, and what's the recovery plan.* Rehearse the bad day before it happens.

### 🧬 Business DNA & Mood
Your business gets a personality profile: Growth Character, Demand Stability, Inventory
Efficiency, Customer Loyalty, Resilience and Growth Potential — each scored out of 100 with
a sentence of proof. Above it, a simple mood face (😊 🙂 😟 😰) with the exact reasons.
Check it monthly; it changes as your business does.

### 📡 Opportunity Radar
The opposite of a risk report — it hunts for **upside**: upcoming festivals (sized from how
much *your own* sales actually rose last year), your strongest weekday, product categories
trending up, and money locked in slow-moving stock. Each card shows the evidence, the
suggested play, and the potential value in ₹.

## 12. Data Center — bring in your real data 📥

**What it does:** upload your real business records from Excel (.xlsx) or CSV files —
products, daily sales, product-level sales, expenses, suppliers, employees.

**How to use it:**
1. Pick what type of data the file contains.
2. Upload the file. The app **reads your column names automatically** ("Item Name" → product
   name, "Qty" → quantity…) and shows you the match to confirm or fix.
3. **Validate** — every row is checked; problems are listed with the row number and reason
   (nothing invalid slips in silently).
4. **Import** — clean rows are added to your twin. Re-importing the same file safely
   *updates* records rather than duplicating them.

The status panel always shows how much of your twin is demo data vs real data.

## 13. Inventory — never run out, never over-buy 📦

- **Restock table:** for every product — days until it runs out, the date to reorder by,
  and a suggested order quantity. Sorted by urgency.
- **Fast movers:** your top sellers — protect their stock first.
- **Slow movers:** products tying up money on the shelf — candidates for a clearance offer.

## 14. Financials — where the money goes 💰

Twelve months of revenue, expenses and profit; an expense breakdown chart (goods, payroll,
marketing, rent); your cumulative cash flow over 30 days; return on spending; and your
**break-even point** — the monthly revenue you need before profit begins, with a clear
above/below indicator.

## 15. Customers — who keeps you in business 👥

New vs returning customer trends, retention and churn rates, average bill size, and
estimated **customer lifetime value** (what a typical customer is worth over a year).
Includes customer segments (Loyal / Regular / Occasional / New / At-Risk) — these are
group-level estimates from your footfall history, clearly labeled; no individual customer
is tracked.

## 16. Risk Analyzer — problems, ranked ⚠️

Continuously scans your twin for: products about to run out, overstock, negative cash flow,
thin margins, falling sales, dropping footfall, high expenses, and understaffing. Every risk
is ranked (critical → low) and comes with concrete numbers — *"Milk runs out in ~3 days."*

## 17. Recommendations — what to do about it 💡

Turns each detected risk into a prioritized action with the reason, expected benefit and
estimated monthly ₹ value. Use the **Simulate** button to test any recommendation safely
before acting.

## 18. Alert Center — nothing slips by 🔔

All warnings in one place as color-coded cards, including per-product low-stock alerts.
Filter by severity. The bell icon in the top bar shows the live alert count on every page.

## 19. Reports — for your records or your accountant 📄

Daily, weekly or monthly business reports: summary numbers, health scores, detected risks
and recommended actions, laid out like a printable document.
- **Export CSV** → downloads your raw day-by-day numbers for Excel.
- **Print / PDF** → use your browser's print dialog and choose "Save as PDF".

## 20. Settings ⚙️

Your profile, dark/light theme, and all your business details (employees, revenue,
expenses, hours). **Keep these roughly up to date** — the simulator calibrates itself
against them when you have little history.

1. **Log in with the demo account** and open the **Dashboard** — get familiar with the tiles.
2. Open the **Digital Twin** and click a red shelf.
3. Go to the **Simulator** — drag price +10%, watch the prediction, then click the

5. Visit the **AI Lab**: slide the **Time Machine** back 90 days, glance at the **Weather**,

7. Ask the **Advisor**: *"What should I do today?"*
8. When you're ready for real data: create your own business in the Setup Wizard and start
   with **Upload** or **Manual** mode.

## 22. Common questions

**Are the predictions guaranteed?**
No — and the app never pretends they are. They are calculated estimates from your own
history, always labeled *"Predicted, not guaranteed"*, always showing their reasoning and a
confidence level. Use them the way you'd use an experienced advisor's opinion.

**What's the difference between DEMO and LIVE data?**
Demo data is realistic sample data so you can explore instantly. It is always labeled DEMO.
The moment you import or add real data, the label changes — the app never mixes them up
silently.

**Will the AI change anything without asking me?**
No. The only feature that *applies* changes is AI CEO Mode, and it acts only when you click
**Approve**.

**I imported a file and some rows were rejected. Is my data broken?**
No — the validation report lists exactly which rows had problems (a bad date, a negative
price…) and why. Fix those rows in your file and re-import; existing records update
in place.

**How do I start over with fresh demo data?**
Ask your administrator to reset the database — the demo account and its full year of data
are recreated automatically.
